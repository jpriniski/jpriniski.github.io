I am a data scientist and PhD student in computational cognition at UCLA researching how cognitive mechanisms, language, and social networks govern what people believe (advisors: Keith Holyoak, Hongjing Lu). My research uses a variety of methodologies to study these processes, ranging from computational cognitive modeling, behavioral and network-experiments, surveys, and Big Data analyses of internet behavior. I'm also interested in how Large Language Models represent causal information in language, and applying them to study how humans reason about causal relation in naturalistic contexts.

I've published empirical, computational, and data science work on topics ranging from the psychological mechanisms of science denialism (A Darkening Spring: Priniski & Holyoak, 2022), conspiracy thinking in online social networks (Rise of QAnon: Priniski, McClay & Holyoak), and developing crowdsourcing strategies to correct misconceptions at scale (Priniski & Horne, 2018, 2019). I'm also exploring new ways to communicate cognitive and data science work to public audiences through alternative publishing models and digital art.
