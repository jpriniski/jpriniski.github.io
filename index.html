Research Overview

I am a PhD student in Computational Cognition at UCLA studying higher-order cognition and human-computer interaction. I use behavioral experiments, computational cognitive modeling, and methods from computational social science, like Big Data analyses of natural language and internet behavior, to understand how cognitive mechanisms and social networks affect what people believe. A few relevant projects include attitude change on Reddit, the mechanics of conspiracy thinking and social activism on twitter, the psychological underpinnings of science denialism, and advancing NLP methods for computational social science.

Alongside my PhD, I am a Contributor and Graduate Student Researcher at Harmony.ONE where I research (and help build) Zero-Knowledge (ZK) web3 applications under Hakwan Lau. With the help of many others, we recently kickstarted zku.ONE, a 7-week course designed to train web3 developers with the necessary skills to build dApps with ZK.

These two research threads allow me to think more carefully about the future of human behavior in digital environments.
