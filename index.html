Research Overview

I'm a fourth-year PhD student in Computational Cognition at UCLA. My advisors are Keith Holyoak and Hongjing Lu and I also work closely with Zach Horne at the University of Edinburgh and Fred Morstatter at USC's Information Sciences Institute. My research uses computational cognitive modeling, behavioral experiments, surveys, and Big Data analyses of internet behavior to understand how rational cognitive processes and social networks create and can correct misconceptions. I've published empirical and data science work on topics ranging from the mechanisms of science denialism (A Darkening Spring: Priniski & Holyoak, 2022), conspiracy thinking on online social networks (Rise of QAnon: Priniski, McClay & Holyoak), and crowdsourcing effective educational interventions (Priniski & Horne, 2018, 2019).
