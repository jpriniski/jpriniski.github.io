I am a scientist cowboy developing human-centered social networks and artificial intelligence. I received my PhD in Computational Cognition at UCLA, working primarily as a member of the Reasoning Lab (PI Dr. Keith Holyoak) and the Computational Vision and Learning Lab (PI Dr. Hongjing Lu). My PhD research integrated experiments on individuals and networked groups, open-source software development, and large-scale analyses of social media to understand how people's beliefs are influenced by interactions in online environments. I actively apply my research to advocate for technology and urban development that harnesses, rather than limits, our individual and collective agency.

I believe that practical public education about how social media, artificial intelligence, and car-centeric urban development isolate us is key to restoring the vitality of our communities and country. I will soon be set free to roam the American West, fighting the ceaseless expansion of development and the scientization of society and human life. I seek collaboration with like-minded individuals who share a sense of urgency in countering these imperial forces. Please reach out if you would like to connect (priniski @ ucla dot e d u).
