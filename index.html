Research Overview

I am a PhD student in Computational Cognition at UCLA studying higher-order cognition and human-computer interaction. I use behavioral experiments, computational cognitive modeling, and methods from computational social science, like Big Data analyses of natural language and internet behavior, to understand how cognitive mechanisms and social networks affect what people believe. Projects include attitude change dynamics on Reddit, the mechanics of conspiracy thinking and social activism on twitter, the psychological underpinnings of science denialism, and advancing NLP methods for computational social science.
