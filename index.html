I am a data scientist and PhD student in computational cognition at UCLA. I work primarily as a member of the Reasoning Lab, where I'm advised by Keith Holyoak and Hongjing Lu. My research integrates experiments with individuals and groups in social networks, computational modeling with Bayesian models and Large Language Models, and data science analyses of online behavior to understand how causal language processes shape reasoning in individuals and social networks. I apply this research to understand why people endorse misconceptions and to recommend strategies for correcting them at scale.

I've published empirical and data science reserach on the Bayesian mechanisms of science denialism (A Darkening Spring: Priniski & Holyoak, 2022), conspiracy thinking in online social networks (Rise of QAnon: Priniski, McClay & Holyoak, 2022), and developing intervention strategies based on Reddit discussions (Priniski & Horne, 2019). I also explore new ways to communicate cognitive and data science work to public audiences.
