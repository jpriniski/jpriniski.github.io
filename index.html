Research Overview

I am a PhD student in Cognitive Science at UCLA studying higher-level cognition. My research integrates experimental design with a wide-array of computational methods to investigate how basic psychological processes give rise to and shape people's conceptual understanding of the world. To this end, I apply computational cognitive modeling, naturalistic language modeling with transformer networks, social network analyses, and brain-computer interfacing with virtual reality to understand the cognitive mechanisms underlying the structure and dynamics of people's belief systems, from their computational elements to how they shape public discourse.

Projects

  • Computational models of analogy and causal inference
  • Belief change in the wild
  • Moral framing of social issues
  • Echo chamber dynamics and conspiracy thinking
  • Lay-science reasoning
  • Advancing topic modeling for computational social science
